Case 01

Subway

"Sub da Quebrada"

Dentsu Creative · 2022

Branding Cultural Strategy Effie Shortlist

Context

Subway had become just another fast-food chain. Disconnected from real life, talking only about products. A partnership with Gerando Falcões — a Brazilian social organisation — emerged as an opportunity to reconnect with culture through something lasting.

The Problem

The sandwich was inspired by peripheral street culture, but wasn't born from it. The quebrada (Brazil's periphery) doesn't need more brands speaking on its behalf.

Insight

The quebrada doesn't need more brands talking about it. It needs space to show its own collective, economic and creative power.

Strategy

Instead of using Subway's product to enter the quebrada, we inverted the model: we made the quebrada cross the bridge — giving real residents voice and space across every Subway store, every TV spot, every social post.

Recognition

Effie Awards Brazil Shortlist. A campaign that didn't just launch a sandwich — it changed who owned the conversation.

Case 02

Kwai

"BBBom Pra Kwai"

BETC · 2021

Brand Strategy Entertainment Effie Shortlist

Context

Kwai, a short-video platform, shifted from sponsoring A Fazenda to becoming the main BBB (Brazil's biggest reality show) sponsor. The challenge: shake off the perception that Kwai was an older, less cool platform.

The Problem

The BBB audience already had an impression of Kwai as the platform that "goes with A Fazenda, not BBB." We needed to break that association and build relevance fast.

Insight

People think they know what kind of content Kwai has. But only those inside the platform know how much more space and depth it gives to entertainment.

Strategy

Turn Kwai into the hub for BBB content, memes and extended conversation. The concept "BBBom Pra Kwai" — a play on a Brazilian expression — tied everything together, showing that on Kwai, BBB gets much more volume.

Recognition

Effie Awards Brazil Shortlist. The campaign ran across the full BBB season, sustained by activation moments that followed the programme's interest curve.

Case 03

Sazón

"Pipoca Neles"

Dentsu Creative · 2022

Campaign Strategy Cultural Insight

Context

World Cup year. Every brand in Brazil was investing heavily in sponsorship and national team content. Sazón's popcorn seasoning had neither the budget nor the product fit to compete in that territory.

The Problem

Popcorn and football live in different universes. Football belongs to barbecue and beer. Popcorn belongs to pop culture and entertainment — not the game itself.

Insight

In their pursuit of the sixth World Cup title, Brazilians root just as hard for their rivals to fail as they do for Brazil to win.

Strategy

Rather than competing in the crowded Brazil-wins narrative, we embraced the rival-fails conversation — the most entertaining, most Brazilian, and most popcorn-appropriate territory of the entire tournament.

Outcome

A campaign that made Sazón relevant during the World Cup without a single sponsorship coin — by owning the conversation no other brand was brave enough to have.

Case 04

BMW

M3 Competition Launch

Ogilvy Consulting · 2021

CRM Strategy Luxury 100% sold in 3 days

Challenge

Launch the BMW M3 Competition in Brazil — a high-performance, limited-availability luxury vehicle — exclusively through CRM, without mass media support.

Approach

Deep consumer profile analysis to identify the right segments within BMW's existing base. Precision messaging mapped to each stage of the purchase journey — building desire before availability, urgency at launch, exclusivity throughout.

Strategy

Treat the CRM channel not as a distribution tool, but as an experience in itself — one worthy of the vehicle's positioning.

Result

100% of the BMW M3 Competition stock sold through CRM alone, in 3 days. No mass media. Pure strategy, precision targeting, and the right message to the right person at the right moment.

Case 05

Volkswagen

Nivus Pre-Launch

AlmapBBDO (Proximity) · 2020

CRM Strategy Automotive 17% conversion

Challenge

Launch the Volkswagen Nivus — a new SUV model — through a pre-sale CRM campaign, converting existing leads into buyers before the car was physically available in dealerships.

Approach

Redesign of VW's CRM base architecture and lead qualification rules. Construction of a full lead journey — from awareness to urgency to conversion — with automated messaging sequences built in Salesforce.

Strategy

Build desire through scarcity and belonging — making pre-launch buyers feel like insiders, not just early shoppers.

Result

17% conversion rate. Entire pre-launch stock sold out in 22 minutes. One of the most successful automotive CRM launches in Brazil at the time.

How I Work

Step 01

Diagnose
the real problem

Most briefs describe symptoms, not problems. Before any strategy, I go upstream — to understand what's actually blocking the brand from where it needs to be. The diagnosis is the strategy.

Step 02

Find the
human truth

Strategy only works when it's rooted in how people actually think, feel and behave — not how we'd like them to. I look for the tension between brand truth and human truth. That gap is where the insight lives.

Step 03

Build the
brief that unlocks

A great brief gives the creative team two things at once: freedom and focus. It opens a space wide enough to surprise, and clear enough to know when the idea is right. That's the craft I've spent 10 years developing.
